Project Snapshot

From Static Menu Site to Booking Engine

The original website looked acceptable but did not guide visitors to reserve. Key booking actions were buried, event nights lacked dedicated pathways, and menu pages were too dense for fast mobile decisions.

  • Before: buried reservation controls and weak event visibility.
  • After: clear reservation hierarchy, event modules, and intent-led navigation.

Homepage Strategy

Built for Fast Dining Decisions

The first viewport now answers three immediate questions: what this place is known for, how to reserve quickly, and what to expect tonight. Supporting sections are arranged by guest behavior, not internal business structure.

Menu Architecture

Meal-Intent Navigation

Menu content was restructured by lunch, dinner, and specials to reduce scanning friction. We introduced visual anchors and quick-jump controls so visitors could get confidence in under 15 seconds.

Event Engine

Campaign-Ready Event Modules

Each weekly event now has a dedicated homepage strip and supporting mini-landing layout. This let social traffic arrive on a page that directly reinforced date, offer, and reservation CTA.

Post-Launch Iteration

CTA Label and Position Testing

After launch, we adjusted reservation button copy and repeated it at key decision points. The final structure reduced hesitation and improved conversion speed on high-intent mobile traffic.
